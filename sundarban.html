Sundarbans, Bangladesh

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest mangrove forest in the world. Located in the delta region of the Padma, Meghna, and Brahmaputra river basins, it is home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger and a rich diversity of flora and fauna. Its serene beauty and unique ecosystem make it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.

Why Visit Sundarbans?

  • Home to the Royal Bengal Tiger and other wildlife.
  • Explore the largest mangrove forest in the world.
  • Experience the tranquility of rivers and canals.
  • Witness breathtaking sunsets over the delta.

Top Attractions in Sundarbans

Karamjal Wildlife Sanctuary

A popular spot for wildlife sightings, including deer, monkeys, and crocodiles.

Hiron Point

Known for its stunning views and frequent tiger sightings.

Kotka Beach

A serene beach surrounded by dense mangrove forests, perfect for relaxation.

Travel Tips for Sundarbans

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Sundarbans is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry.

Local Customs

Respect the natural habitat and avoid littering. Follow the guidelines provided by forest officials.

Safety Tips

Always travel with a guide and avoid venturing into restricted areas. Carry insect repellent and wear comfortable clothing.

Sample Itineraries

3-Day Itinerary

  • Day 1: Arrive in Sundarbans, visit Karamjal Wildlife Sanctuary, and enjoy a boat ride.
  • Day 2: Explore Hiron Point and Kotka Beach. Spend the night on a boat or at a forest rest house.
  • Day 3: Take a guided jungle walk and return with unforgettable memories.

7-Day Itinerary

  • Days 1-3: Follow the 3-day itinerary.
  • Days 4-5: Visit nearby islands and explore more wildlife spots.
  • Days 6-7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Sundarbans before heading back.
